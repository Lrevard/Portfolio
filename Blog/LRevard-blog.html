Discovering Video Games

Growing up, I was first introduced to video games by watching my father and older brother play games like Deus Ex, Baldur's Gate, and Final Fantasy. Seeing an interactive story filled with a mysterious world you're able to explore seemed almost mystical, more than reading it in a book.

Playing video games gave a feeling of accomplishment. Seeing that game over screen with credits unfortunately brought the story to the end; however, seeing the main character and his companions experience hardships and how they persevered and eventually brought their journey to a successful end, fills the player with satisfaction similar to reading one in a good book

Why Video Games Inspire Me

Video games are capable of telling many kinds of stories, some sad, some happy, and most with lessons to learn. Video games can also become a way to learn and discover new ideas or concepts, some with creative avenues like Minecraft, which holds a sandbox element, allows for the player to create things like villages or castles.

Some video games are capable of multiplayer opportunities enabling players from across the country or world to interact with each other, potentially past language barriers.

Video game Fun fact:

The video game industry makes more money than the film and music industry combined, making it one of the largest entertainment sectors globally.

The Future of Gaming

“Death can have me when it earns me”– Kratos, God of War: Ragnarok

Ultimately video games are still a type of media that is still evolving, technology has advanced rapidly with computers allowing for video games to collect an even more lifelike appearance.Things like Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality are still being researched and tested, allowing for different ways to experience the stories told in video games.
